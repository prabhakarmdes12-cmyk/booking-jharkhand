The Queen of Chotanagpur

Perched at 1,128 meters, Netarhat is the highest point of the Chotanagpur plateau. Once a summer retreat for British governors, it remains an untouched paradise of pear orchards, pine forests, and dramatic sunsets that have inspired poets and travellers for generations.

park

Pine Forests

Stunning tall-tree corridors lined with pine and sal, perfect for morning walks and birdwatching.

filter_hdr

Sunset Points

The famous Magnolia Point and Koel View Point offer horizon-wide views that stretch for miles.

Unmissable Attractions

Pear Orchards

Wander through rows of local pear trees during the harvest season and taste the juiciest pears of Jharkhand.

Lower Ghaghri Falls

A hidden 100-ft waterfall nestled deep within the lush forest valley, accessible via a scenic trek.

Netarhat Dam & Sunrise Point

Perfect for a quiet morning picnic or a reflective lakeside walk with panoramic sunrise views.

Frequently Asked Questions

How far is Netarhat from Ranchi? expand_more
Netarhat is approximately 154 kilometers from Ranchi. The drive takes about 4 hours through scenic winding roads and lush green forests.
What is the best time to visit Netarhat? expand_more
October to March is the best time, with pleasant daytime temperatures and cool evenings. Monsoon (July-September) brings lush greenery but roads can be slippery.
Are there good accommodation options in Netarhat? expand_more
Yes, Netarhat Forest Retreat offers premium cottages and suites. There are also budget guesthouses and the Jharkhand Tourism hotel near the bus stand.
Is it safe to drive to Netarhat at night? expand_more
We recommend completing your journey before sunset. The road has several hairpin bends and passes through forest areas, best navigated in daylight.
